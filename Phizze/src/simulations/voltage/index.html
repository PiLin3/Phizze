Voltage

Voltage is defined as the pressure caused by a power source, that pushes charged electrons along a circuit path creating current.

In low resistant materials (Conductors), free electrons like to jump around between atoms randomly. Voltage is what's used to force / push those electrons to move along a path, creating electrical current.

Voltage is measured in Volts (V). One Volt equals one joule of energy produced by one coulomb of charge.

You might hear Voltage referred to as "Potential Difference", the term is referring to the potential difference between the two points in a power source, where one point has a higher potentail and the other has a lower potential the difference between the two points (measured in Volts) is what causes the electrons to move from the higher potential to the more comfortable lower potential point.

Example:

If we took a look at a 1.5 volts battery, the number 1.5 would mean that potential difference between the battery's negative and positive terminals is equal to 1.5 volts, "meaning that if you somehow managed to grap one coulomb of charge from the positive terminal and move it to negative one that action would require you to do 1.5 joules of work, and so now if you released that coulomb from the negative terminal it will try to reach the lower potential (Positive terminal) and that would generate 1.5 joules of energy.

Voltage = V = Joule / Coulomb
