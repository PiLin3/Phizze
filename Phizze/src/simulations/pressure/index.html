About Phizze

Phizze.com is a free educational platform that provides interactive simulations, visualizations, and a multitude of physics, mathamtics, and engineering resources.

Pressure

Pressure is defined as the perpendicular applied force over a surface area ( P = F/A ).

We can see from the pressure equation that pressure is simply the distribution of force over the area on which that force is acting on "perpendicularly". Also we notice that pressure is proportional to the amount of applied force, and inversely proportional to the surface area, meaning that if we increase the applied force while keeping the area the same, then we will increase the pressure, and if we increase the surface area on which that force is acting, while not changing the force, we will reduce the pressure, and vice versa.

The Unit for pressure is Pascal (Pa = N/m2) in SI, or Pound per square inch (psi = lbf/in2) in Imperial / US.

Example:

To give an example of how pressure can vary, imagine trying to hammer a wide cylinder into a wall, which would be a very difficult thing to do, now imagine if you tried to hammer a nail into the wall with the same force, you will most likely be able to push it through easily, the reason is for the cylinder the force is distributed over a large surface area and will exert little pressure on the wall, while in the case of the nail the force is more concentrated at the tip of the nail and therefore can exert more pressure on the wall.

To get an in depth feeling of how Pressure, Force, and Area are related, you can check the simulation and see the effects of changing the applied force or the surface area on pressure.
